Dumbbell Press

The dumbbell Press is a weightlifting exercise performed on a flat bench, typically using a dumbbells. It primarily targets the muscles of the upper body, including the chest (pectoralis major), shoulders (deltoids), and triceps. It also engages the muscles of the back, core, and arms to a lesser extent.

Lat Pulldown

The Lat Pulldown is a back exercise performed on a cable machine. By pulling a wide-grip bar down towards the chest, it targets the lats and upper back muscles. It engages the shoulders, arms, and upper body, making it an effective exercise for overall upper body strength and development.

Deadlift

The deadlift is a compound exercise used for strength building and muscle growth. It is a weight lifting exercise that involves lifting “dead weight” off the floor without the use of momentum, hence its name. The muscles worked in the deadlift include the quadriceps, glutes, adductor magnus, hamstrings, erectors, lats, traps, rhomboids, abdominals, and obliques.

Rows

Rows are a type of strength training exercise that work the back muscles. There are various types of rows, including seated cable rows, T-bar rows, bent over rows, single-arm dumbbell rows, and TRX rows. Rows can be performed using different equipment, such as a seated row machine, cable machine, barbell, dumbbell, or suspension band.

Tricep Pushdown

Tricep extensions are exercises that repetitively flex the elbow joint against resistance to target the tricep muscles. The triceps extend, or straighten, the elbow, as well as aid the shoulder muscles to extend the arm. The tricep extension exercise is an isolation exercise because it involves movement in a single joint.

Bicep Curl

Bicep curls are weight training exercises in which a person bends their arm towards their body at the elbow in order to make their biceps stronger. The bicep curl mainly targets the biceps brachii, brachialis, and brachioradialis muscles. The degree of forearm rotation affects the degree of muscle recruitment between the three muscles.

Overhead Press

The overhead dumbbell press is an upper-body weight training exercise that involves pressing a weight overhead while standing or seated. It is mainly used to develop the anterior deltoid muscles of the shoulder. The overhead press can be performed using a barbell, dumbbell, or kettlebell.

Forearm Curls

Forearm curls are exercises that target the muscles in the forearm. Forearm curls can be performed using dumbbells and include supinated wrist curls, pronated wrist curls, neutral wrist curls, dumbbell pronation, hammer curls, reverse curls, Zottoman curls, dumbbell holds, and finger curls.

Squats

Squats are a compound exercise that primarily targets the lower body muscles, including the glutes, hips, quads, hamstrings, and calves. Squats are performed by lowering the hips from a standing position and then standing back up.
